*🇬🇧 English translation follows the Japanese text below.*

いまヘッドクォーターズには一番若い人で16歳高1男子が、多くは20代の男女が、そして外国人ではハンガリーのカップルも暮らしています。

住人が普段楽しそうに暮らしていたとしても、それが親御さんとかに伝わっているのかな、シェアハウスの暮らしぶりに不安もたれてないかなとふと心配になりました。  
みんなに聞いてみたところ、けっこうみんなハウス内の写真を親に送っていたり、また意外と家族を呼んでハウス内を案内したことがあるそうで、安心しました😊。屋上を見せたらおばあちゃんがすごく喜んでたとかもｗ。

そして海を渡ってきたハンガリアンズは、実はYouTubeで楽しいクリスマスの動画メッセージを作っていたって。許可をもらって転載。みんなでハンガリー語の歌を歌わされたのはこのためだったんですねｗ。いいですねこういうの！

Right now at The Headquarters, our youngest resident is a 16-year-old first-year high schooler, most others are in their 20s, and among our international residents we've even got a couple from Hungary living with us.

Even though everyone here seems to be having a great time day to day, it got me wondering: do their parents actually know that? Are the families of our residents left worrying about what life in a share house is really like?

So I asked around, and it turns out a lot of people are sending photos of the house to their parents, and quite a few have even brought family members by for a tour. That put my mind at ease 😊. Apparently one resident's grandma was thrilled when she saw the rooftop, haha.

And here's the sweetest thing: our Hungarian residents, who came all the way across the sea to join us, had actually put together a fun Christmas video message on YouTube. With their permission, we're sharing it here. So that's why everyone got roped into singing a Hungarian song, haha. Love this kind of thing!
