世間のシェアハウスはそれぞれに色があるというか、はっきりとコンセプトが決まってるものにせよ住民によって自然にうまれた傾向にせよ、それなりに特徴がありますよね。

ヘッドクォーターズの特徴のひとつは、ハウスが買い物に便利な商店街の中に立地してることもあって自炊する人が多いということでしょうか。オーナーも自炊推しなので調理器具はけっこう買い揃えてあると思います笑。

最近ハウス内で作られていた料理をいくつか紹介します。

①シフォンケーキ：  
水道筋イチのポジティブ・ハイテンションガールを目指してるAちゃんは入居時は料理未経験でしたがハウスに来てからは料理をたくさん覚えています。先日は正月休み中に急にケーキを作りたいと言い出し、奮闘していました。勢いあまってデコレーションが変なことになってましたがｗ、いつも飲みにいってるバーに差し入れに持っていってました。😆

②圧力鍋で煮込んだ牛スジカレー：  
年明けに開かれた新年会兼新住民歓迎会兼1月誕生日会（長いっｗ）で作りました。  
牛スジ肉を大量にぶちこみ、じゃがいもは皮付きのまま入れて、そしてトマト缶とカレールーとビーフシチューのルーを混ぜて使い（オイスターソースが隠し味）、圧力鍋で30分煮込んで作りました。時短なのにジャガも人参もスジ肉もとっても柔らかくできました。美味しい楽だし安いしパーティに最高です😆

③低温調理器による肉料理：  
近年流行りの低温調理器。タンパク質の凝固温度を下回る温度で調理するため、お肉がしっとり柔らかに仕上がります。  
グラム198円の豚ヒレと、グラム68円の鶏胸肉で試してみたら見事にごちそうになりました。  
豚ヒレ肉はワインとフォドボーとバターを煮詰めたソースで、鶏胸肉はネギ生姜胡麻油ソースで。これも最高でした😊

Every share house out there has its own color, doesn't it? Whether it's a clear, intentional concept or just a vibe that grew naturally out of the residents living there, each one ends up with its own distinct personality.

One of the things that makes The Headquarters stand out is that, since the house sits right in a shopping arcade that's super convenient for grocery runs, a lot of residents end up cooking for themselves. The owner is big on home cooking too, so we've actually got a pretty solid stash of kitchen equipment stocked up haha.

Here are a few dishes that have been made in the house lately.

① Chiffon cake:
A-chan, who's aiming to become the most positive, high-energy girl on Suidosuji, had zero cooking experience when she moved in, but she's picked up so many recipes since joining the house. The other day, she suddenly decided she wanted to bake a cake during the New Year holidays and threw herself into it. She got a little carried away and the decoration came out looking kind of wild lol, but she still brought it along as a gift to the bar she always goes drinking at. 😆

② Beef tendon curry, slow-cooked in a pressure cooker:
This one was made for the new year party / welcome party for new residents / January birthday party (all rolled into one, quite a mouthful lol) held right after New Year's.
We piled in a huge amount of beef tendon, tossed the potatoes in skin and all, mixed canned tomatoes with curry roux and beef stew roux (with a splash of oyster sauce as the secret ingredient), then simmered it all in the pressure cooker for just 30 minutes. Even with that short cooking time, the potatoes, carrots, and tendon all came out beautifully tender. Delicious, easy, and cheap — perfect for a party. 😆

*   ![Beef tendon curry served at a house party](https://theheadquarters.jp/wp-content/uploads/2020/01/IMG_1743-768x1024.jpg)
    

③ Meat dishes made with a sous vide cooker:
Sous vide cookers have really taken off in recent years. Since the meat is cooked at a temperature below the point where proteins firm up, it comes out incredibly moist and tender.
We tried it out with pork tenderloin at 198 yen a gram and chicken breast at 68 yen a gram, and both turned into an absolute feast.
The pork tenderloin was served with a sauce reduced from wine, fond de veau, and butter, while the chicken breast got a green onion, ginger, and sesame oil sauce. This was amazing too. 😊
