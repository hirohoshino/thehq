５Fの照明を変えてみた  
ハウスに手を入れるのはどうしても何かが壊れたとか問題があった時の修繕が多くて、雰囲気が変わるようなものはなかなか出来て無かったんだけど、久しぶりにやってみたよ

これはみんなが一番集まる５Fのリビング。リビングはシェアハウスのコミュニティとしてのコアだから居心地が大事。前にソファのレイアウトやカバーを変えたけど、照明はずっと以前からあったオフィス型の蛍光灯ベース（富士形といわれるタイプ）で殺風景だったんだよね

それをオシャレな感じにしてみました。👇️これが。。。

👇️こう！

照明はLEDのエジソン電球型の照明と、壁を間接照明的に照らすスポットライトを使ってみました。次のようなやつです。

We changed the lighting on the 5th floor  
When we work on the house, it's usually because something broke or there was a problem to fix, so we rarely got around to doing anything that actually changes the vibe. But this time we finally gave it a shot after a long while.

This is the 5th floor living room, where everyone gathers the most. Since the living room is the core of the share house community, how comfortable it feels really matters. We'd already changed the sofa layout and covers before, but the lighting had always been this old office-style fluorescent fixture (the kind called a "Fuji shape") that made the place feel pretty bare and uninviting.

So we gave it a stylish makeover. 👇 Here's the before...

![Before: the old fluorescent office-style light fixture on the 5th floor](https://theheadquarters.jp/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/IMG_5834-1024x768.jpg)

👇 And the after!

![After: warm pendant lights hanging from a duct rail on the 5th floor](https://theheadquarters.jp/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/IMG_5974-1024x768.jpg)

We removed the fluorescent light and its base, installed a duct rail, and hung a bunch of warm-colored bulbs from it.  
The photos were taken a bit differently so it's hard to compare directly, but hopefully you can still feel the change in mood (if I'd known, I would've tidied up the lamp covers before shooting, lol).

For the lighting itself, we used LED Edison-bulb-style lights along with spotlights that indirectly light up the walls. Something like these.

![LED Edison-style bulb used for the new lighting](https://theheadquarters.jp/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/619ql3HhAJL._AC_SL1500_-760x1024.jpg)

![Spotlight used to indirectly illuminate the walls](https://theheadquarters.jp/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/61eFCCbN2PL._AC_SL1500_-1024x1024.jpg)

Personally, I think warm-toned light feels more relaxing than cool white light and makes parties more fun too. The living room lights can be dimmed and color-adjusted with a remote (which is why they were a bit pricey, lol).  
We've had Christmas parties and candle-talk nights that residents came up with before, so now we can lower the brightness and set the mood for that kind of thing too.

The kitchen got the same treatment... 👇 Here's the before

![Before: the old fluorescent lighting in the kitchen](https://theheadquarters.jp/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/IMG_5839-1024x768.jpg)

And the after!

![After: new warm lighting installed in the kitchen](https://theheadquarters.jp/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/IMG_5972-768x1024.jpg)

Hopefully this makes cooking more enjoyable for everyone.

Honestly, this is something I'd wanted to do for ages, but it always felt like a hassle so I kept putting it off... Turned out the fluorescent base came off easily, and while I was wondering how to go about installing the rail, I found out there's a small part called a "hanger" that lets you reuse the existing suspension bolts — so it actually went pretty smoothly. Though we did need to have someone with the proper qualifications handle the electrical wiring.  
Should've done this way sooner, lol.

![Installing the duct rail and lighting](https://theheadquarters.jp/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/IMG_5966-768x1024.jpg)
